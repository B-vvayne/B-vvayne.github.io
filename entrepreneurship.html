Market Research

Keeping in mind the biopsychosocial paradigm of health1, the roots of every disease lie not only in biology, but in social, psychological and environmental aspects. Therefore, it is important to understand the context of existing treatment options and to raise awareness of our solutions and prevention, where knowledge of social, economic, political, cultural behaviors related to the disease can bring new external insights to the implementation of product design. This way, we can customize our prototypes so that they function optimally and meet the needs of the end user.

Because market research requires less funds and time, it is a work that can help us quickly understand the market and determine the advantages of product positioning. Therefore, in the early stage, we conducted extensive investigation and research on the market conditions, competitors' behaviors and our own products, and we realized that our project has advantages and huge application market, but also has many defects.

Problem Statement

Reports of food poisoning caused by Staphylococcus aureus are numerous, and Staphylococcus aureus accounts for about 25% of foodborne microbial food poisoning incidents2. Its drug-resistant strain, MRSA, can cause severe illness in the gut and is a focus of infection control worldwide.
Due to drug resistance, the treatment of MRSA has many limitations. The current treatment methods are mostly antibiotics, such as vancomycin, teicoranin, linezolid, etc. Linezolid and vancomycin are relatively expensive, and long-term use may cause bone marrow suppression. Teicoranin has lower otorenal toxicity than vancomycin, but its effect is slow, so it cannot become a first-line drug for severe infections3. Given the many limitations, the clinical need for a safe and effective treatment of MRSA is unmet and is an area of potential new drug development.
After background investigation, we believe that the current treatment for MRSA should be in the direction of improving treatment effect, reducing medical costs, and reducing side effects. The super eco we are designing has the potential to meet these points, and it has a huge application prospect.

In order to make our GM drugs accepted by more people, we should strive to achieve good therapeutic effect and ensure their safety through a variety of biosafety measures.

Dominance Discovery

After doing the above three parts of the work, we found that super eco has a unique advantage.
First of all, in terms of cost, due to the high killing efficiency of super eco against MRSA, we only need a small amount of bacteria, which greatly saves costs. Secondly, in the biosafety module, we adopted a biphasic steady-state switch, which does not activate the suicide mechanism in the production process and effectively reduces waste. In terms of production and transportation, the enteric capsules we adopted are simple and easy to produce, and the current lyophilization process is mature, and the storage and transportation costs are low. A combination of factors will result in super eco's final price tag being lower than most existing treatments.
Secondly, safety, we design biosafety modules based on different safety purposes, from transcription of gene pathways to protein synthesis, to suicide mechanisms at the individual level, and finally to quorum sensing mechanisms in populations. A wealth of regulatory tools to ensure that our drug system does not impose additional burdens on the human body; At the same time, the selection of the only probiotic in E. coli as a carrier also greatly reduces the possibility of heterogenic colonization, reducing the risk of bacteria escaping to other parts of the body. Super eco also has the potential to apply for over-the-counter medicines, making treatment easy and safe for patients at home. direction of improving treatment effect, reducing medical costs, and reducing side effects. The super eco we are designing has the potential to meet these points, and it has a huge application prospect.

Product

Product Introduction

Capsule live bacteria is the main product of our team. After taking capsules, super eco will gradually dissolve into the intestine, secrete antimicrobial peptides when encountering MRSA, accurately target MRSA to kill, prevent disease deterioration, and also play an effective preventive role to reduce the risk of infection and avoid zoonosis. We position the product as a health care product and recommend users to take it every once in a while. Escherichia coli is a probiotic naturally existing in human body, which can maintain nutrient absorption, maintain intestinal flora balance and inhibit intestinal bacterial proliferation. If users have intestinal flora disorders such as staphylococcus aureus enteritis and other symptoms, the product can also play a certain therapeutic effect. Competitive Product Analysis

Currently, anti-MRSA drugs commonly used in clinic include vancomycin, teicoranin and linezolid. We compared these three drugs with atropine, which is used to relieve symptoms, in terms of price, application method, side effects and whether resistance will occur. Atropine cannot completely treat MRSA, while vancomycin and Teicoranin are both at risk of causing resistance. Linezolid is a new type of drug, the way of action is completely different from antibiotics, the treatment effect is good, but the treatment is expensive. Super eco can make up for many deficiencies. It has a low production cost, uses antimicrobial peptides to kill MRSA, and has good therapeutic effect without drug resistance. Through the analysis of competitive products, we found the Unique Selling Proposition of super eco: High lethality will not produce resistance at the same time, the cost is low.

The Creativity of Super Eco

Market potential：

With the gradual evolution of microbial pathogens and the overuse and misuse of some drugs, antimicrobial resistance (AMR) has become one of the major public health threats of the 21st century. Globally, MRSA directly caused more than 100,000 deaths in 2019,

According to GlobalData, a data analytics and consulting firm founded in 1999, the MRSA treatment market will experience limited growth over the next decade, growing from $1.4 billion in 2014 to $145 million in 2024. Many of these new products will establish niche positions in MRSA therapeutics and will also realize their potential outside of MRSA therapeutics.

Solve the problem of unequal medical resources：

A major challenge in tackling drug-resistant bacteria is understanding the true burden of resistance, especially in areas where surveillance is minimal and data scarce. However, until now, no comprehensive evaluation study covering all regions and a wide range of pathogens and drug combinations has been published. This is why we are concerned about the vast rural areas, the hidden corners of many people suffering from illness.

Super eco's production costs are lower, and we plan to adopt an intermediate pricing strategy to eliminate competitors while achieving a fair profit margin. In the future, we also plan to develop a series of supporting facilities such as sensors to detect types of bacterial food poisoning and give the right medicine, which will help us to provide low-cost supporting products for people who do not want to pay too much for treatment. Super eco's live bacteria capsules are easier to promote than injectable liquid, and the publicity form of health care products is conducive to its acceptance by the public. People with bad stomach and health care workers who are easily exposed to MRSA environment are our audience, especially in rural areas. Low price and convenient use form are easier to spread, overcome backward medical resources, and protect everyone's intestinal health in the most harmless way.

While high-income countries also face alarmingly high levels of antibiotic resistance, its impact is most severe in low - and middle-income countries, highest in sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia, at 24 per 100,000 and 22 per 100,000, respectively. In sub-Saharan Africa, AMR indirectly causes 99 deaths per 100,000 people; In South Asia, AMR is indirectly responsible for 77 deaths per 100,000 people4. We hope that by working with foreign governments, regional and international organizations, super eco can help them as well.

In the future, we plan to cooperate with the government to promote super eco to grassroots hospitals, pharmacies, and health centers to help more areas lacking medical resources and alleviate the problem of inequality.

Promote employment：

We plan to set up a start-up company, as the company continues to grow, it will directly bring employment in the development, and drive indirect employment through its influence on the upstream and downstream of the industrial chain. In the middle stage of development, the company's scale expands rapidly, and the demand for jobs rises. In the later stage of development, the company implements a steady expansion strategy, and the production, sales and operation match. The employment growth we can bring is considerable.

Intellectual Property

Considering the legal protection of the project, we plan to apply for a patent for our drug in China, and when the technology progresses, the patent should be extended accordingly, so as to prove the feasibility of super eco, increase its competitiveness, and make it easier to attract investors and persuade them to cooperate with us. After studying the Patent Law of China, we determined that super eco meets the requirements of the patent of pharmaceutical products, belongs to the invention patent, and has the novelty, creativity and practicability stipulated in Article 22 of the Patent Law. We have manufactured super eco by synthetic biology method, which can effectively kill MRSA. No one had ever patented anything like this before.

At the same time, because our design uses some patented technologies of others, the process of commercialization of the project also involves patent transfer and patent implementation. Patent transfer means that the patentee, as the transferor, transfers the ownership or holding right of its invention and creation patent to the transferee, and the transferee pays the agreed price. In the future, we will consider conducting patent transactions when necessary. You may also consider working with the patentee to obtain a license to enforce the patent.
